## Key Facts Pertaining to Eurogrip
- **35%** revenue from products launched in the last 3 years
- **$3 billion** annual revenue of TVS Mobility Group to be leveraged
- **85+** countries presence
- **10%** share in the Indian 2W tyre market
- R&D excellence with global technological integration

## 1. Customer Pain-Points
- Many customers still associate EUROGRIP with "TVS Tyres," diluting its premium identity.
- Riders report faster wear and inconsistent performance compared to competitors like MRF.
- Poor grievance resolution and low adoption of the "Click to Connect" app hinder customer satisfaction.
- Low brand recall among Gen Z and millennials despite MS Dhoni endorsement.

## 2. Issues Through the Customer Journey Mapping
| Stage | Description | Drop-off |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Low brand recall & weak digital presence | Low awareness |
| Consideration | Lack of trust; mechanics prefer MRF | Quality concerns |
| Purchase | Poor after-sales service | Hesitation in repeat purchase |
| Post-Purchase | Limited engagement, low retention | — |

## 3. Customer Need Identification (Funnel)
**ToFu — New/Non-Engaged Customers**
- Need: Become part of the evoked set in tyre purchase decisions.
- Message: Highlight grip, safety, durability via endorsements, social media, and influencer marketing.

**MoFu — Existing Low Loyalty Customers**
- Need: Make EUROGRIP the first choice for riders & mechanics.
- Message: Address quality concerns with side-by-side durability comparisons, testimonials, and retailer engagement.

**BoFu — Engaged Customers**
- Need: Drive repeat purchases and customer loyalty.
- Message: EUROGRIP Loyalty Program — personalized offers, referrals, post-purchase engagement.

## 4. Eurogrip's Key Challenges
- **Mechanics' Perception (87%):** Most mechanics rank Eurogrip below MRF & CEAT, hurting credibility.
- **Customer Problems (44% vs. 13% for MRF):** Higher dissatisfaction and potential quality concerns.
- **Legacy "TVS Tyres":** Ongoing brand confusion weakens Eurogrip's premium positioning.
- **Market Penetration Gap:** No 4-wheeler presence, weak aftermarket share.

*Based on Pareto analysis of on-field research: Mechanics Ranking (87%) and Customer Problems (44%) are the top brand identity factors.*

## Market Sizing
- **2W Market in India:** Domestic market share (FY24) 75.35%
- **Revenue Expected (FY25):** USD 33.47B (CAGR FY25–29 ≈ 4.24%)
- **Sales Volume Growth (FY23→FY24):** 13.31%
- **2W Tyre Market in India:** Valuation (2024) USD 12.8B (7.4% of global); Projection (2030) USD 29B (CAGR 7–9%); Indian 2W tyre market (2025) ≈ USD 3B (CAGR 10–12%)

### Key Insights by Category
- Mechanics are key influencers in tyre purchase decisions; priorities: grip, stability, durability.
- Segment needs: radial tyres for sports bikes, dual-terrain durability for adventure bikes, high-performance braking for superbikes.
- Preferred attributes: high-quality materials, silica compounds, reinforced sidewalls, tubeless options.
- Brand preference driven by trust, quality, robust distribution/availability, and strong manufacturer support.

## Competitor Analysis

| Brand | Market Share | Technology | Range/Variety | Pricing | Key Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Eurogrip** | Growing (10%) | Innovative — DuCT, TriP, RoBusT | Wide range for commuters & scooters | Value for money | Availability issues; premium models (e.g., Protorq Extreme) hard to find offline |

| **JK Tyre** | Moderate (8%) | Smart Tyre Technology, "Tyre with Brain" | Focus on affordability, popular in budget segment | Affordable | Pricing pressure; marketing weakness & lower recall |
| **Apollo** | Niche (~5%) | Apollo Quiet Tyre Technology, WAV Range for EVs | Good mix of commuter & performance | Moderate/expensive | High price perception; high dependence on Indian market |
| **Michelin** | Niche (~5%) | REGENION Technology, Advanced Materials | Premium, best for longevity & grip | Expensive | Premium pricing; limited domestic market presence |

**Top 3 Performing Brands**
- **TVS Eurogrip:** Leading tyre supplier to OEMs, strong in the fast-growing EV segment.
- **MRF:** Strong brand legacy and prestigious symbol.
- **CEAT:** Excellence in Total Quality Management (TQM).

**Perceptual Mapping:** Michelin/MRF lead high-performance/high-price; CEAT balances performance and price; Apollo/JK Tyre sit lower on price and brand image; Eurogrip needs to move up on brand image despite reasonable availability.
